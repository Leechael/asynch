Pool contention (D)
-------------------

Workers: 1, 8, 32, 128. Pool maxsize: 8, 32. Each worker performed five
SELECT 1 loops per round. The latency proxy injected RTTs 0, 1, 5, and 20 ms.
The fixed criterion is: at high contention, maxsize median throughput changes
by at most 10% and the measured median is within [0.5x, 2x] of 1/(2*RTT).

RTT 0 ms: not assessed (the theory is undefined without injected RTT).
  Median aggregate ops/sec for workers 32: maxsize 8=891.391, 32=887.931.
  Median aggregate ops/sec for workers 128: maxsize 8=866.883, 32=872.797.

RTT 1 ms: not established under the fixed criterion.
  Workers 32: maxsize 8=186.937 ops/sec (0.374x theory),
  maxsize 32=187.553 ops/sec (0.375x theory), a 1.003x gain.
  Workers 128: maxsize 8=190.389 ops/sec (0.381x theory),
  maxsize 32=187.216 ops/sec (0.374x theory), a 0.983x gain.
  Maxsize does not help, but the sub-millisecond-adjacent proxy/operation cost
  keeps throughput below the criterion's lower factor bound.

RTT 5 ms: established.
  Workers 32: maxsize 8=74.381 ops/sec (0.744x theory),
  maxsize 32=74.223 ops/sec (0.742x theory), a 0.998x gain.
  Workers 128: maxsize 8=74.694 ops/sec (0.747x theory),
  maxsize 32=74.509 ops/sec (0.745x theory), a 0.998x gain.

RTT 20 ms: established.
  Workers 32: maxsize 8=23.486 ops/sec (0.939x theory),
  maxsize 32=23.582 ops/sec (0.943x theory), a 1.004x gain.
  Workers 128: maxsize 8=23.483 ops/sec (0.939x theory),
  maxsize 32=23.568 ops/sec (0.943x theory), a 1.004x gain.

Conclusion: the lock-inside-RTT bottleneck is established for the 5 ms and
20 ms injected-RTT measurements. The evidence is about Pool scheduling across
connections only. It does not claim protocol-level multiplexing or concurrent
queries on one connection.

Conclusion: event-loop blocking is established for all measured A scenarios on
this host. Lowering max_block_size from 65409 to 8192 has no consistent,
material p99 improvement: several cases improve slightly, while others are
flat or worse. This supports the measured motivation to evaluate yielding at
block boundaries after that change exists; it does not itself change driver
behavior. Compressed String-heavy asynch reads are the most disruptive measured
workload (stream p99 271.366 ms at 65409, 268.049 ms at 8192).
